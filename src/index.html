Cybersecurity and Auditing

17:50 - 18:00

Authors
Rimsha Riaz, Pedro Carneiro

Cyber Security Audit Compliance in the Banking Sector: A Comparative Study of Pakistan and Europe

This study examines the cybersecurity audit compli- ance frameworks in the banking sectors of Pakistan and Europe, highlighting their distinct approaches, strengths, and challenges. In Europe, regulatory frameworks such as the GDPR, NIS2 Directive, and ISO 27001 ensure comprehensive compliance, fos- tering robust cybersecurity measures across the financial sector. Conversely, Pakistan’s frameworks, guided by the State Bank of Pakistan and standards like ISO 27001 and PCI DSS, are still evolving, facing hurdles such as resource constraints and limited technical expertise. Through a comparative analysis, this paper identifies key differences in regulatory maturity, adoption rates, and implementation challenges. Recommendations are proposed to enhance cybersecurity audit compliance, including fostering collaboration, leveraging emerging technologies, and investing in capacity-building initiatives to safeguard financial systems from emerging cyber threats.

18:02 - 18:12

Authors
Diego Santos Alvarez, Silvestre Malta, Pedro Filipe Cruz Pinto

Assessing the Implementation of the EU 5G Toolbox Recomendations: A Pan-European Study on Cybersecurity Measures in 5G Networks

The rapid deployment of 5G networks across Europe presents significant cybersecurity challenges that must be addressed to ensure the integrity of critical digital infrastructures. The EU 5G Toolbox provides strategic and technical recommendations to mitigate risks and strengthen the resilience of 5G networks. This article highlights the importance of these recommendations by analyzing key documents and related studies, focusing on the challenges and best practices in implementation by European Union Member States. The findings emphasize the role of collaborative measures in reducing cyber threats and promoting a secure 5G ecosystem.

18:14 - 18:24

Authors
José Dias

Security in Network Slicing in 5G: Challenges, Attack Taxonomy, and Mitigation Strategies

This paper explores the security challenges associated with network slicing in 5G networks, a technology that enables the creation of virtual networks tailored to different use cases. It presents a detailed taxonomy of specific attacks, categorizing vulnerabilities across layers such as orchestration, virtualization, and inter-slice communication. Additionally, mitigation strategies are discussed, emphasizing the importance of real-time monitoring and robust access controls. This study contributes to a deeper understanding of threats in network slicing, providing a solid foundation for future research and developments in the security of 5G networks and beyond.

18:26 - 18:36

Authors
Joel Alves

Cybersecurity Analysis of Communication Protocols in Home Automation Devices: A Theoretical Investigation of Vulnerabilities and Solutions

The rapid growth of the Internet of Things (IoT) has revolutionized home automation by enabling seamless integration and communication among smart devices. However, this advancement has introduced significant cybersecurity challenges due to vulnerabilities in communication protocols such as Zigbee, Z-Wave, Wi-Fi, and MQTT. This paper investigates these vulnerabilities, focusing on their alignment with the ETSI EN 303 645 standard, and highlights the challenges of practical implementation. By exploring cryptographic advancements, AIdriven intrusion detection systems, and compliance tools, this study proposes actionable solutions to enhance the security and resilience of IoT-enabled home ecosystems. The findings aim to bridge the gap between theoretical standards and realworld implementations, fostering a secure and user-centric smart environment.

18:26 - 18:36

Authors
Aurora Vasconcelos, Silvestre Malta, Emanuel Peres, António Rio Costa

Zero Trust: A Comparative Analysis of the USA and European Contexts

This paper analyzes Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA) adoption in the USA and EU, exploring regulatory, cultural, and technical factors influencing implementation. It highlights the distinct challenges each region faces, particularly European complexities due to stringent GDPR regulations. Recommendations for region-specific frameworks supporting Zero Trust implementation are provided.

Cryptography

18:50 - 19:00

Authors
João Rosa, Luís Barreto

Assessment Analysis of Cryptographic Tools

This thesis presents both an assessment analysis and a bibliometric and bibliographic analysis of cryptographic tools. The bibliometric and bibliographic analysis examines the evolution, trends, and key research contributions within the field. Given the rapid growth of cryptography due to escalating cybersecurity threats, these analyses aim to provide valuable insights into the development and future directions of cryptographic research, enhancing our understanding of its critical role in modern cybersecurity solutions. Meanwhile, the assessment analysis focuses on evaluating the strengths, weaknesses, and effectiveness of various cryptographic tools to support secure digital communication and information protection.

19:02 - 19:12

Authors
Anderson Sales

GitEncryptor: An Encryption and Privacy Tool for Securely Sharing and Storing Files on GitHub

GitEncryptor is a Python-based encryption tool that secures sensitive files on GitHub using AES-256 encryption. It ensures data confidentiality and integrity, allowing users to retain control over access keys. The tool addresses risks related to leaked GitHub API keys and provides a robust solution for data protection in public and private repositories.

Attacks and Detection

19:45 - 19:55

Authors
Pedro Ferraz

Automating Sequence Attack Detection in APIs Through Dynamic Workflow Generation

API security is one of the main challenges in modern software architectures, especially with the proliferation of microservices. Sequence attacks, which exploit dependencies between different endpoint calls, pose a significant threat but are often overlooked by dynamic security scanners (DAST). This work proposes an approach to generate dynamic workflows that allow the automated execution of tests against these types of attacks. The solution uses collections of endpoints, such as those exported by Postman or defined in OpenAPI, to create call sequences that simulate real attack scenarios.

19:57 - 20:07

Authors
André Alves, Silvestre Malta, Pedro Filipe Cruz Pinto

A Dataset for Machine Learning-Based CSRF Attack Detection

This article introduces a specialized dataset designed for analyzing and detecting Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF) attacks using machine learning. By including diverse traffic patterns and capturing HTTP request characteristics, the dataset serves as a foundational resource for future CSRF research and detection solutions.

20:09 - 20:19

Authors
Elton Moreno, Joel Vaz

Brokenwire: An Attack on Fast EV Charging Stations

Brokenwire is an attack that exploits vulnerabilities in the Combined Charging System (CCS), enabling remote disruption of electric vehicle (EV) charging sessions. This study outlines the technical aspects of the attack and highlights the need for enhanced security measures in CCS to protect against potential widespread disruptions.

20:21 - 20:31

Authors
Danilo Oliveira

Vulnerability of Intel SoCs Running Intel's Modified MINIX 3 in Ring - 3

This paper investigates the CVE-2017-5689 vulnerability in Intel's Management Engine (ME) built on a modified MINIX 3 microkernel. The vulnerability allows remote attackers to gain full system control due to improper handling of null passwords. Mitigation strategies and insights into the security implications of Intel's ME architecture are discussed.

20:33 - 20:43

Authors
Hugo Costa, Ricardo Santos, Silvestre Malta

A Comprehensive Taxonomy of Data Poisoning Attacks: Strategies, Impacts, and Mitigation Techniques

This paper explores data poisoning attacks in machine learning, their impact on model performance, and mitigation techniques. It establishes a taxonomy for attacks targeting model integrity and availability, emphasizing the need for secure data collection practices. This foundational resource advances understanding and defense mechanisms against data poisoning.

Phishing and Social Engineering

20:45 - 20:55

Authors
Elton Moreno, Joel Vaz

Research into Susceptibility to Phishing and Social Engineering Techniques

Susceptibility to phishing and social engineering attacks remains a significant cybersecurity challenge, especially among young people with frequent access to digital platforms. This study used an online survey, with simulated scenarios and questions related to security practices, to assess behavior and identify areas of vulnerability in young Cape Verdeans with higher education, or in the process of doing so. The results suggest an encouraging level of awareness of digital risks, with many participants demonstrating safe practices, such as validating the authenticity of messages before interacting. However, greater vulnerability was found in messaging scenarios. This indicates the need for interventions to address behavioral gaps and strengthen the digital resilience of this group.

20:57 - 21:07

Authors
António Fernando Braga Lima

Análise do Impacto do Phishing no Ambiente Escolar: Um Estudo de Caso com Professores

The growing digitization of school environments has brought benefits to education but also expanded the attack surface for cybercrimes, such as phishing. This study investigates the impact of phishing on teachers’ digital behavior, highlighting existing vulnerabilities and proposing solutions to mitigate risks in school environments. Conducted at Escola Profissional de Vila do Conde, the research included questionnaires, interviews, and phishing simulations. Results showed that cybersecurity knowledge levels are a determinant in exposure to phishing attacks, emphasizing the urgent need for continuous training programs and robust institutional policies.

Artificial Intelligence and Cybersecurity

21:25 - 21:35

Authors
José Machado

Temporal Provenance Analysis in Deepfake Videos

The rise of deepfake technology has introduced challenges to maintaining the authenticity of digital media. This paper presents a methodology for Temporal Provenance Analysis as an approach to deepfake detection. By analyzing inconsistencies in video temporal metadata and anomalies in frame sequences, it uncovers patterns that signify manipulation. Supported by tools like FFmpeg, OpenCV, and Python libraries, the study aims to set the stage for future research in deepfake detection.

21:37 - 21:47

Authors
António Martins

An Analysis of the Impact of AI on Organizations with a Focus on Security

This paper examines the organizational impact of Generative Artificial Intelligence (GAI) on security. It highlights benefits like improved threat detection and risks such as data breaches and regulatory compliance challenges. The study aims to develop a framework for secure GAI adoption, addressing data privacy and ethical considerations.

21:49 - 21:59

Authors
João Marques

Artificial Intelligence in Industry 5.0: Challenges, Opportunities, and Cybersecurity Perspectives

This paper examines AI's role in Industry 5.0, emphasizing human-machine collaboration for industrial resilience and cybersecurity. Challenges such as data dependency, integration barriers, and reliability issues are addressed. Emerging technologies like Edge Computing and Federated Learning are discussed as solutions to enhance AI's transformative potential in secure industrial operations.

Emerging Technologies and Infrastructures

22:01 - 22:11

Authors
Marco Moreira

Assess Memory Isolation Mechanisms Used on Cloud Providers

This research evaluates the memory isolation mechanisms of cloud providers to ensure secure and isolated environments for virtual machines. By developing scripts to analyze memory dumps, the study identifies potential cross-VM data contamination and assesses the effectiveness of current isolation mechanisms. Insights aim to improve memory security practices in virtualized cloud environment.

22:13 - 22:23

Authors
Francisco Ferreira, Sérgio Lopes

ForensIIoT: A Framework for Forensic Analysis in IIoT Environments

Industrial IoT (IIoT) environments pose unique challenges for cybersecurity due to their heterogeneous nature, limited computational resources, and increasing attack complexity targeting critical infrastructures. This paper introduces ForensIIoT, a centralized framework for automated log collection, integrity assurance, and evidence management in IIoT environments. Through simulated attack scenarios and performance evaluations, ForensIIoT demonstrates its ability to improve incident response, forensic readiness, and compliance with regulatory standards. This framework represents a significant step toward robust and scalable forensic methodologies for industrial settings.
